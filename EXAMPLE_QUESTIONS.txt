## Overview / Descriptive Questions
- How many records and features are in the dataset?
- What are the types of variables (numerical, categorical, dates)?
- What are the summary statistics for numerical features (mean, median, min, max)?
- How are categorical variables distributed (counts per category)?
- Are there missing values or nulls? How prevalent are they?
- Are there duplicate records?
## Trends / Temporal Questions
- Are there trends over time (daily, weekly, monthly)?
- Which periods have the highest or lowest activity/outcome?
- Do patterns change seasonally or over years?
- Are there spikes or drops that need explanation?
## Comparison / Grouping Questions
- How do different groups compare on key metrics?
- Do certain categories perform better than others?
- Are there differences by location, age, or demographic factors?
- How does one variable change with another?
## Correlation / Relationships
- Are there correlations between numerical variables?
- Which features are most strongly associated with outcomes of interest?
- Are there unexpected relationships or anomalies?
- Can we predict an outcome based on other variables?
## Segmentation / Clustering
- Can we group records with similar characteristics?
- Do certain clusters stand out in performance, behavior, or outcomes?
- What are the defining features of each cluster?
## Outliers / Anomalies
- Are there extreme values that affect analysis?
- Do anomalies reveal interesting patterns or problems?
- How should outliers be handled (keep, remove, or investigate)?
## Actionable / Decision-Oriented Questions
- What insights can a stakeholder act on?
- Which variables drive key outcomes?
- Are there groups that need intervention or attention?
- What recommendations can be made based on the analysis?
- How could insights be implemented to improve outcomes?
## Quick tip for brainstorming
Take any dataset and ask yourself:

1) Who is interested in this data? (student, customer, player, patient, etc.)
2) What problems or goals do they have?
3) What patterns, trends, or correlations would help them decide something?

This usually leads to 5–10 specific questions that can guide your notebook.
